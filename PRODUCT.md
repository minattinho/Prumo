# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Contractors (contratantes):** Homeowners, building managers, and small business owners in Brazil looking for vetted local service professionals. They're skeptical — they've been burned by unreliable hires, fake reviews, and hidden fees. They want to see real work before committing, and they want to contact the professional directly.

**Professionals (prestadores):** Skilled tradespeople (electricians, plumbers, architects, painters, etc.) who want visibility without paying per-lead or per-job commissions. They're proud of their work and want a place to show a real portfolio. They're not tech-savvy by default — onboarding must be friction-free.

## Product Purpose

Prumo is a Brazilian local service marketplace that connects contractors with verified local professionals. The core promise: real portfolios, real reviews, direct contact — no intermediaries, no commissions, no bullshit. Professionals pay a flat monthly subscription (R$79/mo after 30 days free) to maintain their verified profile.

Success looks like: a contractor finding a plumber in their city, seeing 8 real job photos and 12 verified ratings, and calling them directly in under 2 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Confiável e direto. Like a skilled tradesman you'd recommend to a friend — no hype, no hidden agenda, just proven work. Practical, honest, grounded. Speaks plainly (Portuguese, informal "você" register). Doesn't oversell. Confident without arrogance.

Three words: **honesto, direto, confiável**.

## Anti-references

- **GetNinjas / Habitissimo:** Cluttered marketplace UI, cheap-looking visual hierarchy, commission-heavy feel — Prumo's transparency is the opposite
- **Generic SaaS (Intercom/Notion-style landing pages):** Gradient blobs, floating UI mockups, purple/teal palettes, investor-pitch tone — Prumo is local and human, not a funded startup product
- **OLX / Mercado Livre:** Classifieds-grid aesthetic, high visual noise, low-trust feel — Prumo is curated, not a free-for-all listing board

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't claim.** Real portfolio photos beat trust badges. Real ratings beat "verified" icons used decoratively. Let the professional's work do the talking.
2. **No friction between contractor and professional.** Every screen should reduce the number of steps to direct contact. If an element doesn't serve that path, question its presence.
3. **Earned confidence, not manufactured credibility.** Design that looks like it's trying hard to look trustworthy is the opposite of trustworthy. Restraint and clarity over reassurance theater.
4. **Built for the job site, not the boardroom.** Copy and visual language should feel accessible to someone who spends their days in work boots, not in front of a monitor. Plain language, clear labels, no jargon.
5. **Local first.** This is a marketplace for real neighborhoods. Design decisions should reinforce proximity, community, and real human connection — not scale and abstraction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Focus on mobile accessibility (large portions of both user groups access on mobile). Sufficient color contrast on all text. Consider users with limited digital literacy — error messages must be clear and actionable, not technical.
